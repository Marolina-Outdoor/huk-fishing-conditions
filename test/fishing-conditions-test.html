How the Fishing Conditions widget works

It answers three questions fast: Should I go? When should I go? What do I need? It pulls live weather, marine, and tide data for your location, scores how favorable conditions are right now (0–100), and explains why — then points you to a license and gear.

The Cast Score (0–100)

The score is a weighted blend of the conditions that actually move fish. Each factor is scored 0–100 on how favorable it is, then combined by weight. Coastal weights:

FactorWeightWhat scores best
Water Temp18%Sweet spot ~65–84°F; fish slow below 55°F
Tide movement17%Within ~2 hrs of high/low; slack water is slowest
Current15%~0.5–1.5 kt — enough to move bait
Wind14%~5–15 mph; over 25 mph shuts it down
Pressure trend12%Slowly falling, or stable high
Surf10%Calmer is safer/clearer nearshore
Sky / light8%Overcast & partly cloudy beat full sun

A few realism caps keep the score honest: thunderstorms cap it at 20; sustained wind over ~21 mph or surf over 4 ft caps it in the 80s; rapidly swinging pressure or two-plus weak factors pull it down. So an "88" really means most things lined up.

Each condition card

💨 Wind Speed & direction. Light to moderate is ideal for casting and boat control; calm-glassy can spook fish, strong wind ends the day.
🌊 Tide Incoming / outgoing / slack and the next high/low time. A moving tide pushes bait and triggers feeding.
🔄 Current Estimated water movement. Enough flow keeps bait active without ruining presentation.
🌡️ Water Temp Sea-surface temperature. Drives fish metabolism and where they hold.
🏄 Surf Wave height nearshore — affects access, safety, and water clarity.
📊 Pressure Barometric pressure and its trend. Falling pressure ahead of a front often sparks a bite.
👁️ Sky Cloud cover & conditions. Lower light generally means fish feed more freely.
ℹ️ "Why?" icon Every card has an info icon that opens its sub-score and the ideal range, so the number is never a black box.

Coastal vs. Inland

The widget auto-detects your water. If you're near the coast it runs in Coastal mode (tide-driven, with current & surf). If there's no tide station nearby — a lake, river, or reservoir — it switches to Inland mode:

Coastal Tide chart + next high/low, plus Wind, Current, Water Temp, Surf, Pressure, Sky.
Inland A Solunar feeding-times timeline replaces the tide chart. Cards become Pressure, Solunar, Wind, Sky, Moon, Air Temp.

Solunar theory: fish feed hardest during major windows (moon directly overhead or underfoot, ~2 hrs) and minor windows (moonrise & moonset, ~1 hr). We compute these from the moon's position for your exact location and date, and weight them alongside pressure, wind, and sky.

Where the data comes from

Open-Meteo — weather, wind, pressure, cloud cover, marine waves & sea-surface temperature, and the 7-day outlook. NOAA CO-OPS — tide predictions for the nearest station. Computed astronomy — moon phase and solunar windows (no third-party service). OpenStreetMap/Nominatim — turns your coordinates into a state for the right fishing-license link.

Scores are guidance, not a guarantee — conditions and fish both have a sense of humor.
